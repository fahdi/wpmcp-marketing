Why safety matters for SEO work
01

SEO mistakes are invisible until they are expensive.

A wrong price shows up in the next order. A wrong noindex or a broken canonical shows up weeks later, as traffic that quietly stopped coming. By then, nobody remembers which of the 300 edits did it.

02

Bulk work is exactly where agents earn their keep, and where they do the most damage.

Rewriting metadata across a whole site is the perfect agent task: repetitive, rule-driven, boring. It is also one prompt misreading away from a site-wide mess. The answer is not to avoid bulk edits. It is to make them reversible.

03

Every edit logged, every session one click from undone.

Each metadata write is snapshotted through the same engine that protects every WP MCP change, and grouped into its session. Review the log, restore one post, or roll back the entire bulk run at once.

What the agent can actually do

Metadata, links, and context. Nothing invented.

These are the real ability names the agent calls, registered through the official WordPress Abilities API. WP MCP writes SEO metadata through the plugin you already run, Yoast SEO or Rank Math, using their own fields, so nothing changes in your SEO setup except who is doing the typing. The full reference documents each one.

What this is not

WP MCP is not a rank tracker, a keyword research tool, or an audit-score dashboard. It is the layer that lets an agent do real SEO edits on your site safely. Your SEO plugin keeps doing its job; the agent just gets careful hands.
