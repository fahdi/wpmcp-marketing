Use of the plugin on your site

WP MCP runs entirely on your own WordPress installation and connects to an AI client of your choosing over the Model Context Protocol, authenticated by a WordPress application password you control. You are responsible for:

  • Which AI client you connect, and what permissions that connection carries.
  • Backups of your own site, independent of WP MCP's snapshot/rollback engine, which is a change-recovery tool, not a full site backup solution.
  • Complying with the terms of any AI provider (for example Anthropic or a Cursor-compatible client) you connect to your site.
  • Reviewing which tools are enabled and which capabilities they require before granting write access on a production site.

What the safety engine does and does not guarantee

WP MCP's snapshot-and-restore engine covers a specific, documented set of object types: posts and pages (including WooCommerce products and menu items, which are posts under the hood), options, users, comments, and WooCommerce orders. A small set of deliberately risky operations, raw database row writes and permanent plugin/theme/file deletion, are disabled by default and, where enabled, honestly report when they are not recoverable rather than claiming an undo path that does not exist. Free-tier operation history is capped at the most recent 20 operations; a long AI agent session on the free tier can outrun that cap before it finishes. See the documentation for the current, precise scope; we would rather you read exactly what is and is not covered than assume it covers everything.
